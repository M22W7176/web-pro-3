• Galle Fort

    Undoubtedly the most iconic structure of our sunny south, the Galle Fort is a 17th century UNESCO World Heritage Site that effortlessly blends history with the modern-age. While its colonial ramparts stand tall above the Indian Ocean, its interior is lined with cobblestone streets filled with quaint boutiques and restaurants that also make for wonderful walking tours.

  • Mask Museum

    Mask making is a popular tradition in Sri Lanka. Many Sri Lankan mask artisans ply their trade along the Western, South Western and Southern coasts of the island. Of these, the Ambalangoda area on the South Western coast of Sri Lanka is renowned for its talented artisans.
